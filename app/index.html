    Speakers

    Sara Chipps

    @sarajchipps

    Sara Chipps is a JavaScript developer based in NYC. She’s the CTO of Flatiron School, her free time she works on wearables for http://kittiebots.com and hacks on Nodebots.

    Vanessa Hurst

    @DBNess

    Vanessa Hurst is the founder and CEO of CodeMontage, where coders engage in open source projects to improve their skills and the world. She founded Developers for Good and co-founded WriteSpeakCode and Girl Develop It.

    Jen Myers

    @antiheroine

    Jen Myers is a web designer/developer and part of the instructor team at Dev Bootcamp in Chicago. She speaks regularly about design, development and diversity, and focuses on finding new ways to make both technology and technology education accessible to everyone.

    Katie Miller

    @codemiller

    Katie Miller is an OpenShift Developer Advocate at Red Hat, where she tries to squeeze as many different programming languages into her workdays as possible. She is also a Co-Founder of the Lambda Ladies group for women in functional programming and one of the organisers of the Brisbane Functional Programming Group

    Mark Dalgleish

    @markdalgleish

    Mark Dalgleish is a senior developer at SEEK in Melbourne, Australia and the lead organiser of MelbJS. He’s obsessed with everything web and loves using JavaScript, CSS and HTML to create rich experiences that resonate with end users.

    Silvia Pfeiffer

    @silviapfeiffer

    Silvia is an open video technology enthusiast with a long background in open source software, media technology R&D, and in open standards. She is the author of “The Definitive Guide to HTML5 Video”. Silvia is now working for NICTA on new applications using WebRTC.

    Sarah Hui

    @sehsarah

    shui.js – an elegant, lightweight framework for all your JavaScript needs, powering cutting edge cloud-based point of sale at Vend in Auckland.

    Toni Barrett

    @toni_b

    Toni owns Beauty Bliss - an online retail store based in Wellington. Originally a side project, Beauty Bliss has evolved into a full time job with Toni working on everything from product development to the code base. Before making the move to self-employment, Toni was a front end developer at StarNow and BBC Worldwide.
